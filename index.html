A little about me

Greetings! I am an ambitious and dedicated individual with a passion for education, leadership, and community engagement. Currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Economics with a minor in public policy at New York University Abu Dhabi, I have consistently sought opportunities to learn, grow, and make a positive impact. Through a diverse array of roles, internships, and initiatives, I have honed my skills and contributed meaningfully to various projects. My journey in education began at New York University Abu Dhabi in August 2020, where I embarked on a pursuit of knowledge in Economics with a minor in public policy. This academic endeavor has provided me with a strong foundation in economic principles and policy analysis, empowering me to approach complex challenges with a well-rounded perspective. Prior to my university studies, I completed my School Leaving Certificate (SLC) at Gandaki Boarding School in Pokhara, Nepal in May 2019. This early education laid the groundwork for my future pursuits, instilling in me a drive for academic excellence and a thirst for knowledge.

Some of my Teaching and Education roles

Ready to explore how education, leadership and research can transform lives? Navigate through the sections below to discover my experiences, insights, and vision.

Brooklyn International High School, New York, US

During my time in New York, I volunteered at Brooklyn International High School (BIHS). I supported a science teacher, in executing project-based activities and assisted students with their classwork. BIHS serves a unique student body of refugees and first-generation immigrants, presenting language barriers and additional Social-emotional management needs. Volunteering with immigrant and refugee students, navigating language barriers, and witnessing the power of inclusive education have left a lasting impression on me.

Booster Education Institute, Pokhara, Nepal

As the Founder and Chairperson, I brought together accomplished young Nepali graduates from esteemed universities like Harvard, NYU, and UPenn and designed a comprehensive curriculum for 10th-grade students that merged academic and skill-based learning. As part of our innovative approach, our initiative included engaging activities such as graphics design classes, startup bootcamps, hiking, campus tours, and futsal matches. Notably, we organized a week-long summer camp with diverse sessions led by professionals. The impact of the program has instilled in me the dedication to empowering high school students through holistic programs, shaping their academic and career journeys.

Teaching Intern at Bloom Nepal School, Lalitpur, Nepal

I worked as a full-time teacher at Bloom Nepal School in Lalitpur, Nepal, for a year before I moved to NYUAD. I taught Mathematics and Science to students in Grades 5-8. I emphasize hands-on learning, organizing a science exhibition where sixty students showcased models and experiments and guided senior students to participate in an Inter-school competition. Similarly, I introduced a Network Teaching Approach that grouped 35 residential students into 7 clusters, fostering heightened motivation and engagement. My journey at Bloom Nepal School ignited my passion for impactful education, propelling me forward on this meaningful path.






Some of my Leadership & Community Involvement

Program Coordinator: Youth Leadership Network (YLN) - NYUAD Community Outreach

As the program coordinator of YLN at NYUAD Community Outreach, I spearheaded our efforts to inspire and empower young minds. I led weekly team meetings to meticulously plan and execute five engaging workshops, which included an educational excursion to Qasr AlHosn for a cohort of 120 high school students from 30+ high schools in Abu Dhabi. Guided by a user-centered design philosophy, I played a pivotal role in crafting a curriculum centered around the vital theme of 'Culture Preservation'. My responsibilities extended to the seamless coordination of logistics, communications, and program implementation, ensuring a meaningful experience for all participants.

Founding president of Nepali Student Association at NYU Abu dhabi

I served as the Founding President of the Nepali Student Association at NYUAD. In this capacity, I laid the foundation for a vibrant community by crafting a mission statement and the association’s constitution, securing approval from the Office of Student Life. I strategize long-term objectives including strengthening bonds with alumni and organizing events that resonate with our members' interests. My commitment was evident through weekly E-board meetings where I collaborated with fellow leaders to implement our programming plans. Through this experience, I fostered unity among Nepali students while enhancing my leadership and organizational capabilities.*

First-Year Dialogue (FYD) Facilitator and Weyak Leader

As an FYD Facilitator and Weyak Leader for two consecutive years, I played an integral role within the First Year Experience Student Life team. I actively contributed to the planning and seamless execution of a comprehensive welcome week program, encompassing orientation sessions, stage performances, and overall logistical arrangements. Furthermore, I was entrusted with the responsibility of overseeing a dedicated group of 15 first-year students. As their primary point of contact, I assumed the roles of both mentor and resource person, providing guidance and support throughout their initial year of university life.


Resident Assistant at NYU Abu Dhabi Residential Education, Abu Dhabi, UAE

a multifaceted role as a college resource, social organizer, and policy enforcer for a diverse floor of 34 first-year residents during COVID-19. As an RA, I conceptualized event proposals, led monthly gatherings, and actively fostered a sense of unity within the community. First School Captain of Gandaki Boarding School, Pokhara, Nepal

During my role as the first School Captain of GBS, I led a student body of 1150 and collaboratively worked with the school administration to set a legacy of impactful works. I was part of the planning and implementation of various school programs, club activities, and sports events throughout the academic year. Among my achievements, I successfully devised an annual residential calendar, fostering a sense of structure and engagement within the student community. Under this, I initiated inter-hostel activities such as poetry, table tennis, and basketball competitions. I also started an impactful campaign aimed at reducing food wastage, creating a queue system, and organizing seating arrangements in the dining hall. This experience allowed me to nurture my leadership skills while making a tangible positive impact on the school environment and its community.




Research

Research Intern at Governance Lab, Kathmandu, Nepal

I worked as a Research Intern at the Governance Lab in Kathmandu, Nepal during the summer of 2022. Working closely with a team of esteemed scholars like Prof. Rohini Pande (Yale) and Prof. Michael Callen (LSE), I contributed to launching a field survey that examined the participation of women in local-level elections in Nepal during the years 2017 and 2022. I also helped in the formulation of the survey questionnaire, overseeing translation efforts, conducting rigorous pilot studies, and meticulously managing data. Alongside these responsibilities, I conducted in-depth one-on-one interviews with subjects and co-facilitated training sessions for survey enumerators and back-checkers. This enriching experience allowed me to actively engage in the research process and work towards shedding light on important social and political dynamics within Nepal.*
